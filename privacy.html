AutoSpotter Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

AutoSpotter ("we," "our," or "us") values your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use the AutoSpotter mobile app (available on Google Play). It applies to all users of the app and covers the data collected through the app.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

Account & Profile Data: Information you provide when creating an account, such as your email address, username, name, password (stored securely), and an optional profile photo.

User Content: Data you upload to the app, including vehicle photos, your car collection information (make, model, year, and other specs), and friend connections. This also includes optional data like location associated with car spottings, any captions or comments you add, and optional car videos (note that videos are stored only on your device unless you choose to share them).

Device & Usage Data: Technical data from your device, such as device model and operating system version, to ensure compatibility and diagnose issues. We may also collect usage statistics to improve the app.

App Permissions: The app requests permission to access certain features of your device only when needed to enable app functionality:

  • Camera: To take photos of vehicles (accessed only when you choose to take a photo within the app).
  • Storage: To save and manage your vehicle photos, collection data, and other app files.
  • Location: To use mapping features and tag spot locations (accessed only when you enable location for a spotting).
  • Internet: To synchronize data with online services and enable features like cloud storage and AI processing.
  • Network State: To check if your device is online and adjust app behavior accordingly.

If you grant permissions, you can later change or revoke them through your device settings.

3. Third-Party Services and Partners

AutoSpotter relies on several third-party services to provide functionality. These services may collect and process data as described below (each has its own privacy policy):

  • Firebase (Google): We use Firebase Authentication, Cloud Storage, and Firestore for user accounts, database storage, and secure backend services. (See Firebase Privacy Policy).
  • Google ML Kit: Used for on-device detection and blurring of license plates and faces in photos. (See Google Privacy Policy).
  • TensorFlow Lite (Google): Used on your device for basic car detection; processing is local and no data is sent to servers.
  • Google Gemini API: An AI service for optional car identification (see Gemini API Terms). When used, only the photo you submit is sent to Google for analysis (not stored).
  • Cloudinary: A third-party service for storing and delivering uploaded photos. Cloudinary securely stores your images only to provide reliable access and does not use them for any other purpose. (See Cloudinary Privacy Policy).
  • MapLibre GL: An open-source mapping library used to display maps in the app. It runs entirely on your device and does not send your data anywhere.
  • Advertising & Analytics (Future): We may integrate third-party advertising or analytics services (for example, Google AdMob) in the future. Such services would collect data as described in their own policies. We will update this Privacy Policy accordingly before launching any advertising features.

3.1 AI Car Detection (Google Gemini API)

AutoSpotter offers an optional feature to identify vehicle make/model using Google's Gemini AI. If you choose to use this feature, the app sends the car photo to Google's API. Google's servers analyze the image and return identification results. No personal information is sent; only the image itself is transmitted. The photo is not stored permanently by Google (it is deleted after processing). Each user has a limited number of free detections. All results include confidence scores; please verify the identification for accuracy before relying on it.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Account Management: To create and maintain your user account and profile.
  • Feature Functionality: To enable app features such as processing your photos (applying AI car detection and blurring license plates/faces) and managing your car collection.
  • AI Services: To provide car identification via Google Gemini when you opt in (as described above). We also track usage of this AI feature to enforce any usage limits.
  • Social Features: To support friend connections and sharing between users (e.g., allowing you to view your friends' collections).
  • Mapping and Locations: To enable mapping of your car spottings and related features.
  • Notifications: To send you important updates about the app (e.g., account notifications or updates to the Privacy Policy). You may opt out of non-essential notifications.
  • App Improvement: To analyze and improve the app's functionality, troubleshoot issues, and enhance user experience.
  • Car Specifications Database: We may analyze anonymous vehicle make/model data from our database to improve and verify our car specifications. This process uses only vehicle identification data, not personal information.

5. Data Security and Storage

We take steps to protect your data:

  • Secure Servers: User data is stored on secure servers (e.g., Firebase/Google Cloud) with encrypted transmission (HTTPS/TLS) and encrypted storage at rest.
  • Authentication: Access to your account requires secure authentication (you set a password, which is never stored in plain text).
  • Encrypted Media: Photos you upload are stored securely (e.g., via Cloudinary). Cloudinary's systems are secure and used only to store and deliver images for the app.
  • Local Processing: Sensitive processing (like car detection, privacy blurring, and watermarking) is done on your device. Original images remain on your device unless you upload them. This ensures raw images are not sent to our servers for these tasks.
  • Local Videos: If you record or import car videos, they remain only on your device. These videos are encrypted on the device (using AES-GCM encryption) and never uploaded or shared externally. Videos are automatically deleted if you remove the associated car entry or delete your account.

We follow best practices and implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard your personal data against unauthorized access or disclosure.

6. Data Sharing

We never sell your personal information. Data sharing is limited to the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We share data with trusted third-party service providers who help run the app (e.g., Firebase, Google ML services, Cloudinary). These providers are bound by confidentiality and use your data only to perform services for us. For example, Cloudinary stores your uploaded photos solely to provide access; it does not use them for any other purpose.
  • Advertising (Future): If we implement advertising, we may share certain data with ad networks (as outlined in our updates).
  • Law and Safety: We disclose information if required by law (such as a valid subpoena or court order), or to protect our rights, safety, or property.
  • Friends: If you connect with other users (as friends in the app), we share limited information with them. Friends can see your vehicle collection details and profile photo. However, no precise location data is shared with friends.
  • Public Spot Feed: If you choose to share a photo to the public Spot Feed, the following information will be visible to all users: the photo, your caption, the vehicle's make/model, like count, and the general location (city and country) and your display name/avatar. No exact address or precise GPS coordinates are included. You can disable feed sharing at any time (see Section 9.3).
  • Friend Requests: When someone sends you a friend request through the feed, they can see your display name and profile picture to identify you. No other personal data is shared.
  • Administrative Access: Our administrators can access all user data for moderation, troubleshooting, and support. This is to ensure safety and compliance with our Terms of Service.

6.1 Map and Location Privacy

AutoSpotter's mapping features handle location data carefully:

  • Private Map Display: Only you can see the exact locations of your own car spottings on the in-app map. Other users, including your friends, cannot view your car locations.
  • Location Accuracy: If you enable location for a spotting, we record precise GPS coordinates at the time of the photo. This allows accurate pinning on the map.
  • Friends Access: Connected friends can see your car details (photo, make/model, etc.) but cannot see any location information or map markers.
  • Privacy Controls: All location sharing is off by default. You must explicitly enable location for any spotting. You can control this at any time via app settings or your device permissions.
  • Location Storage: Location coordinates are stored securely in our database (Firebase Firestore) with strict access controls. Only you and our admin team (for maintenance) can access your location data.
  • Mapping Technology: We use MapLibre GL for maps. This open-source library processes all map rendering on your device; no map data or personal info is sent to external servers by the mapping component.
  • Data Removal: If you delete a car photo/entry, its associated location data is also deleted from our servers.
  • Content Moderation: Administrators may view car entries (including locations) for moderation purposes. This ensures reported or problematic content can be checked. Regular users cannot see anyone else's location data.

Privacy Summary: Your exact car locations are private and visible only to you. Friends can view your car collection details and photos but cannot see any location information.

9. Additional (Optional) Features

AutoSpotter offers several optional features with extra privacy considerations:

On-Device AI Detection: The app includes basic car detection and privacy blurring (for license plates and faces) using TensorFlow Lite and Google ML Kit on your device. All processing occurs locally, and original images remain on your device. We do not send your photos to external servers for this feature.

Local Video Storage: If you record car videos within the app, they are stored only on your device in encrypted form (using AES-GCM). These videos are never uploaded or shared externally. Deleting the associated car entry or your account will erase these videos.

Cloud AI Identification: As mentioned in Section 3.1, the optional Google Gemini service can analyze car photos. This is an opt-in feature: you must choose to send a photo to Google for identification. Only the image is sent (no personal data), and it is not stored permanently.

Friends & Collection Sharing: If you connect with other users, you can view each other's car collections and profile photos in the app. Friends cannot see your precise car locations or any additional personal data beyond what you've shared (such as display name and avatar).

Public Spot Feed: (See Section 9.3 below.)

9.1 Photo Reporting System

Users can report photos in the app that violate our Terms of Service or community guidelines. The reporting process works as follows:

  • Reporting Process: You may flag any photo and provide a reason (e.g., offensive content, privacy concerns, etc.).
  • Report Details: When you report, we collect your user ID, the reported photo's ID/details, and the reason you provided.
  • Administrator Review: Our admin team reviews all reports. If a photo is found to violate rules, the appropriate action will be taken.
  • Removal Criteria: Photos may be removed if they are illegal, inappropriate, infringe privacy/property rights, or breach our guidelines.
  • Possible Actions: Admins may delete the reported photo entirely or remove its location data (for privacy) while keeping the photo.
  • User Notification: If your photo is removed or modified due to a report, we will notify you through the app.
  • Privacy of Reporter: Your identity as the reporter is kept confidential. The photo owner will not know who reported them.
  • Retention of Reports: We keep report records for moderation and safety reasons, but we do not use this information for any other purpose.

9.2 Photo Watermarking

All photos processed through AutoSpotter are automatically watermarked for branding and content protection:

  • Watermark Content: A subtle "AutoSpotter" watermark is added to the bottom-right corner of every photo.
  • Processing: Watermarking is applied locally on your device after any privacy blurring and before saving or uploading the image.
  • Purpose: The watermark helps identify content from our app and discourages unauthorized use.
  • Local Processing: This happens on your device; your photo is not sent elsewhere to add the watermark.
  • Non-Removable: Watermarks cannot be turned off. They are a built-in part of the app's photo processing.

9.3 Public Spot Feed

The Spot Feed is a community feature where users can share and browse car sightings:

  • Visibility: If you upload a photo with Feed sharing enabled, it may appear in the public Spot Feed for all AutoSpotter users. Each feed post shows the photo, your caption, the car's make/model, the number of likes, and general location (city and country). Precise GPS coordinates are never shown.
  • Profile Information: Your display name and profile photo (if you have one) are shown alongside your shared photos in the feed. This helps identify who spotted the car. No other personal details are included.
  • Location Safety: Only the city and country of the spot are displayed. Exact location data (street or coordinates) remains private.
  • Interactions: Other users can like your post or report it. Reported posts are handled through the system described in Section 9.1.
  • Opt-Out: You can disable sharing your photos to the feed at any time in Settings → Privacy. Doing so will remove your existing shared posts from the feed and prevent future posts from appearing.
  • Friend Requests: While browsing the feed, users can send you a friend request by clicking the request button next to your post. To facilitate this, your display name and profile photo must be visible.

Note: Posting to the Feed is optional. All users can see public feed posts, but your exact location remains hidden.
