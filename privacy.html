1. Introduction

AutoSpotterX ("we," "our," or "us") values your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use the AutoSpotterX mobile app (available on Google Play). It applies to all users of the app and covers the data collected through the app.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

Account & Profile Data: Information you provide when creating an account, such as your email address, username, name, password (stored securely), and an optional profile photo.

User Content: Data you upload to the app, including vehicle photos, your car collection information (make, model, year, and other specs), and friend connections. This also includes optional location data associated with car spottings (stored only on your device, never uploaded to our servers), any captions or comments you add, and optional car videos (note that videos are stored only on your device unless you choose to share them).

Device & Usage Data: Technical data from your device, such as device model and operating system version, to ensure compatibility and diagnose issues. We may also collect usage statistics to improve the app.

App Permissions: The app requests permission to access certain features of your device only when needed to enable app functionality:

  • Camera: To take photos of vehicles (accessed only when you choose to take a photo within the app).
  • Storage: To save and manage your vehicle photos, collection data, and other app files.
  • Location: To use mapping features and tag spot locations (accessed only when you enable location for a spotting).
  • Internet: To synchronize data with online services and enable features like cloud storage and AI processing.
  • Network State: To check if your device is online and adjust app behavior accordingly.

If you grant permissions, you can later change or revoke them through your device settings.

3. Third-Party Services and Partners

AutoSpotterX relies on several third-party services to provide functionality. These services may collect and process data as described below (each has its own privacy policy):

  • Firebase (Google): We use Firebase Authentication, Cloud Storage, and Firestore for user accounts, database storage, and secure backend services. (See Firebase Privacy Policy).
  • Google ML Kit: Used for on-device detection and blurring of license plates and faces in photos. (See Google Privacy Policy).
  • TensorFlow Lite (Google): Used on your device for basic car detection; processing is local and no data is sent to servers.
  • Google Gemini API: An AI service for optional car identification (see Gemini API Terms). When used, only the photo you submit is sent to Google for analysis (not stored).
  • Cloudinary: A third-party service for storing and delivering uploaded photos. Cloudinary securely stores your images only to provide reliable access and does not use them for any other purpose. (See Cloudinary Privacy Policy).
  • MapTiler: A third-party mapping service that provides map tiles and styling for the app's mapping features. MapTiler processes requests for map tiles but does not have access to your personal location data or car spotting information. (See MapTiler Privacy Policy).
  • MapLibre GL: An open-source mapping library used to render maps on your device. It runs entirely on your device and does not send your data anywhere.
  • Advertising & Analytics (Future): We may integrate third-party advertising or analytics services (for example, Google AdMob) in the future. Such services would collect data as described in their own policies. We will update this Privacy Policy accordingly before launching any advertising features.

3.1 AI Car Detection (Google Gemini API)

AutoSpotterX offers an optional feature to identify vehicle make/model using Google's Gemini AI. If you choose to use this feature, the app sends the car photo to Google's API. Google's servers analyze the image and return identification results. No personal information is sent; only the image itself is transmitted. The photo is not stored permanently by Google (it is deleted after processing). Each user has a limited number of free detections. All results include confidence scores; please verify the identification for accuracy before relying on it.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Account Management: To create and maintain your user account and profile.
  • Feature Functionality: To enable app features such as processing your photos (applying AI car detection and blurring license plates/faces) and managing your car collection.
  • Country Discovery: To power the Country Spots browsing experience, which surfaces publicly shared cars by country using the ISO country code stored with each eligible upload.
  • AI Services: To provide car identification via Google Gemini when you opt in (as described above). We also track usage of this AI feature to enforce any usage limits.
  • Social Features: To support friend connections and sharing between users (e.g., allowing you to view your friends' collections).
  • Mapping and Locations: To enable mapping of your car spottings and related features.
  • Notifications: To send you important updates about the app (e.g., account notifications or updates to the Privacy Policy). You may opt out of non-essential notifications.
  • App Improvement: To analyze and improve the app's functionality, troubleshoot issues, and enhance user experience.
  • Car Specifications Database: We may analyze anonymous vehicle make/model data from our database to improve and verify our car specifications. This process uses only vehicle identification data, not personal information.

5. Data Security and Storage

We take steps to protect your data:

  • Secure Servers: User data is stored on secure servers (e.g., Firebase/Google Cloud) with encrypted transmission (HTTPS/TLS) and encrypted storage at rest.
  • Authentication: Access to your account requires secure authentication (you set a password, which is never stored in plain text).
  • Encrypted Media: Photos you upload are stored securely (e.g., via Cloudinary). Cloudinary's systems are secure and used only to store and deliver images for the app.
  • Local Processing: Sensitive processing (like car detection, privacy blurring, and watermarking) is done on your device. Original images remain on your device unless you upload them. This ensures raw images are not sent to our servers for these tasks.
  • Local Videos: If you record or import car videos, they remain only on your device. These videos are encrypted on the device (using AES-GCM encryption) and never uploaded or shared externally. Videos are automatically deleted if you remove the associated car entry or delete your account.
  • Local Location Storage: GPS coordinates for your car spottings are stored exclusively on your device in encrypted form. Location data is never uploaded to our servers or shared with anyone. Only you have access to your location information.

We follow best practices and implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard your personal data against unauthorized access or disclosure.

6.1 Map and Location Privacy

AutoSpotterX's mapping features handle location data with maximum privacy protection:

  • Private Map Display: Only you can see the exact locations of your own car spottings on the in-app map. Other users, including your friends, cannot view your car locations.
  • Location Storage: GPS coordinates are stored exclusively on your device in encrypted form. Location data is never uploaded to Firebase or any external servers. Only you have access to your location information.
  • Legacy Data: Some older location data from earlier versions of the App may still exist in Firebase. This legacy data is automatically removed when you update that car's location or delete the car entry. The app will continue to work with legacy data until it is updated, at which point it is automatically migrated to local-only storage. If you wish to request immediate deletion of any remaining legacy location data from our servers, you can contact us at zernoxi6@gmail.com.
  • Country Information Only: To enable country-based features (like Country Spots browsing), we derive and upload only the country name and country code (e.g., "United States" and "US") from your coordinates. Your exact GPS coordinates are never uploaded or shared.
  • Friends Access: Connected friends can see your car details (photo, make/model, etc.) but cannot see any location information or map markers. Location data is completely private to you.
  • Privacy Controls: You can disable location attachment at any time in App Settings or through your device permissions. When disabled, location will not be added to new spottings unless you explicitly choose to include it.
  • Mapping Technology: We use MapTiler for map tiles and MapLibre GL for map rendering. MapTiler provides only the map imagery (like a digital map background). MapTiler does not receive your GPS coordinates, car information, or any personal data—only standard anonymous map tile requests.
  • Data Removal: If you delete a car entry, all associated location information is permanently deleted from your device. Any legacy location data in Firebase is also automatically removed when you delete the car or update its location.
  • No Third-Party Access: Your location data is never shared with friends, other users, third-party services, or administrators. It remains encrypted on your device and is accessible only to you.

Privacy Summary: Your exact car locations are stored only on your device and are completely private. Only you can see your location data. Friends can view your car collection details and photos but cannot see any location information. No one else has access to your location data—not our servers, not third parties, and not other users.

9.2 Photo Watermarking

All photos processed through AutoSpotterX are automatically watermarked for branding and content protection:

  • Watermark Content: A subtle "AutoSpotterX" watermark is added to the bottom-right corner of every photo.
  • Processing: Watermarking is applied locally on your device after any privacy blurring and before saving or uploading the image.
  • Purpose: The watermark helps identify content from our app and discourages unauthorized use.
  • Local Processing: This happens on your device; your photo is not sent elsewhere to add the watermark.
  • Non-Removable: Watermarks cannot be turned off. They are a built-in part of the app's photo processing.

9.3 Public Spot Feed

The Spot Feed is a community feature where users can share and browse car sightings:

  • Visibility: If you upload a photo with Feed sharing enabled, it may appear in the public Spot Feed for all AutoSpotterX users. Each feed post shows the photo, your caption, the car's make/model, the number of likes, and general location (city and country). Precise GPS coordinates are never shown.
  • Profile Information: Your display name and profile photo (if you have one) are shown alongside your shared photos in the feed. This helps identify who spotted the car. No other personal details are included.
  • Location Safety: Only the country name (derived from coordinates) is displayed in the feed. Exact location data (GPS coordinates, street addresses, or city names) is never uploaded to our servers and remains private on your device.
  • Interactions: Other users can like your post or report it. Reported posts are handled through the system described in Section 9.1.
  • Opt-Out: You can disable sharing your photos to the feed at any time in Settings → Privacy. Doing so will remove your existing shared posts from the feed and prevent future posts from appearing.
  • Friend Requests: While browsing the feed, users can send you a friend request by clicking the request button next to your post. To facilitate this, your display name and profile photo must be visible.

Note: Posting to the Feed is optional. All users can see public feed posts, but your exact location remains hidden.

9.4 Country Browsing Feature

AutoSpotterX includes a "Country Spots" feature that allows users to browse car sightings organized by country:

  • Country-Level Display: Users can explore public car spots from specific countries (e.g., United Kingdom, United States, UAE, Germany, etc.). This feature shows only country-level location data—no precise GPS coordinates or street addresses are ever displayed.
  • Educational Content: Each country section includes curated descriptions of local automotive culture and popular car spotting locations (e.g., "Sloane Street in London" or "Beverly Hills in California"). This information is provided for educational and community purposes to help enthusiasts discover car spotting opportunities.
  • Public Feed Integration: Only cars that are not marked as hidden from the feed during upload appear in the country browsing feature. If you choose to hide a specific photo from the feed when uploading, it will be excluded from country browsing as well.
  • Information Shown: For each country, users can see car photos and make/model details. Unlike the main feed, usernames and profile information are not displayed in the country browsing view to provide a streamlined browsing experience focused on the vehicles.
  • Rarity Filter: Similar to the public feed, only rare vehicles (RARE+ classification) are shown from users with Auto XP ≤3600. Users with higher XP levels can have any vehicle appear in country browsing.
  • Country Code Storage: To enable this feature, we store an ISO country code (e.g., "US" for United States, "GB" for United Kingdom) with each public spot. This code is derived from your GPS coordinates using reverse geocoding. Only the country-level information is stored—your exact coordinates remain on your device and are never uploaded.
  • Privacy Maintained: The country browsing feature shows even less personal information than the main feed—no usernames, profile photos, or personal details are displayed. Your precise car locations, home address, and all personal data remain fully private.

Purpose: This feature helps users discover automotive culture and rare car sightings in different regions worldwide, while maintaining strong privacy protections for exact locations and user identities.
